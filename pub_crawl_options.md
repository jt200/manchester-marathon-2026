## General Pub Selection Tips

### What to Look For
- ✅ **Direct on marathon route** - you can watch from the pub/street
- ✅ **Outdoor seating/beer garden** - easier to see runners
- ✅ **Food available** - you'll be hungry after jogging
- ✅ **Good atmosphere** - it's a festive day!
- ✅ **Quick service** - get pints fast during busy times

### Areas with Strong Pub Culture
- **Stretford:** Mix of traditional locals and newer bars
- **Sale:** Waterside location, multiple drink options
- **Altrincham:** Town centre is buzzing, easy to find pubs
- **Chorlton:** Trendy neighbourhood, lots of choice
- **City Centre:** Post-race celebration hub, packed

### What to Avoid
- ❌ Pubs NOT on the route (you'll miss her)
- ❌ Interior-only seating (can't watch outside)
- ❌ Too far off the marked marathon path
- ❌ Very quiet pubs (you want atmosphere!)

## Hydration Note
You're jogging ~10+ km on race day. Plan to:
- Drink water at each pub (ask for tap water between pints!)
- Eat something at each stop if possible
- Take it easy on the pace—this is a spectator crawl, not a race
- Bring a small backpack with water, snacks, phone charger
